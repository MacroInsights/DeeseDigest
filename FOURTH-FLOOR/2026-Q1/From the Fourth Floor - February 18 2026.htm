Hello Deese College Community, 

At the Deese College, we take pride in honoring individuals whose impact continues to shape our legacy. I want to take this time to recognize the life and service of the Reverend Jesse Jackson Sr. ’64, civil rights leader, presidential candidate, and proud North Carolina A&T alumnus, who passed on yesterday at the age of 84.

Rev. Jackson transferred to A&T in 1960, where he became student government president and quarterback of the Aggie football team, graduating in 1964 with a degree in sociology. He went on to work alongside Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and became a national civil rights leader, founding Operation PUSH and later the Rainbow/PUSH Coalition. He made history with his groundbreaking presidential campaigns in 1984 and 1988 and received the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2000.

A devoted Aggie, he was a friend to the Deese College and supporter of Dean Emeritus Quiester Craig.  Rev. Jackson showed his commitment to business education as he is pictured above with Dean Craig and the Dean Milton Wilson from Howard University in the 1980s.

Let us continue to exemplify the ideals of our rich Aggie legacy and commit to paying it forward to the next generation.  

Have a great day!

Regards, 

Dean Smith
