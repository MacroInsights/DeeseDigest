Bluford Library Updates
 

Open Access Publishing and Article Processing Charges (APCs)
Due to ongoing funding limitations, the Library has temporarily suspended its Open Access Fund for Article Processing Charges (APCs). While this model is not sustainable at this time, the Library is actively collaborating with academic colleges to explore future support options. Faculty and students are encouraged to utilize publisher discounts and fee waivers available through the Library’s read-and-publish agreements. Information on participating publishers and submission guidelines is available via the Library’s Open Access Guide. For additional assistance, please contact iyanna@ncat.edu or tbrussel@ncat.edu.

Lower-Level Library Innovation and Research Hub
The lower level of the Library now serves as a centralized Innovation and Research Hub, co-locating the Data Science Lab, Center for Teaching Excellence, Faculty Commons, and Instructional Technology Training and Development (ITTD). This integrated space is designed to foster collaboration, support faculty development, promote interdisciplinary scholarship, and advance data-informed research and teaching practices.

Data Science Lab Software Resources
The Data Science Lab, scheduled to open during the last week of January, will offer access to a comprehensive suite of software to support research, instruction, and data analysis. Available tools include: 

  • Adobe Creative Cloud
  • Anaconda3
  • ArcGIS Pro
  • IBM AMOS
  • IBM SPSS Statistics
  • MATLAB
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Google Chrome
  • Power BI
  • Python
  • R
  • RStudio
  • SAS
  • Tableau
  • Zoom

Congratulations!


Dr. Adelle Bish and her research partner’s proposal has been accepted for presentation at the annual meeting of the Society for Industrial and Organizational Psychology (SIOP), to be held in New Orleans from April 29 to May 2. Their research, titled “Feedback Mechanisms: An Unexpected Hero for Improving Inclusion?”, will be presented in the Inclusive Workplace Practices Research Forum as part of a four-hour collaborative session focused on advancing scholarship in inclusive workplace research.
