135th Founders Day Convocation and Installation (Classes Suspended)

 

We are two weeks from N.C. A&T’s 135th Founders Day Convocation and Installation of Chancellor James Martin II on Monday, March 9, beginning at 10 a.m. in Corbett Sports Center. Faculty participation— in full academic regalia—is essential to honoring this historic moment. Classes will be suspended from 8 a.m. to noon.

Please note the following:

  • Faculty must RSVP to attend—RSVP Here.
  • Faculty must be fully robed and in position by 9:30 a.m.
  • The academic procession begins promptly at 9:45 a.m.
  • The University Bookstore only offers regalia rentals during Commencement; faculty should plan accordingly if additional regalia needs must be met.

Let’s show up in full Aggie excellence as we mark a defining chapter in our university’s legacy.

2026 N.C. A&T Faculty Research Symposium

 

The Division of Research invites you to the 2026 Faculty Research Symposium. This year's theme is "Catalysts of Innovation: Faculty Research Driving Discovery, Collaboration, and Economic Impact."

The symposium will incorporate an inspiring address from our distinguished keynote speaker, National Science Board Chair Dr. Victor McCrary, a poster session showcasing funded and unfunded research, a targeted focus on the road to commercialization, and presentations from N.C. A&T Faculty, along with sessions from Sandia National Laboratories, Naval Nuclear Laboratory, and Thermo Fisher, speed networking, splash labs to assist faculty with questions onsite, and more.


Please RSVP by Monday, April 6, 2026, below!

Note: The RSVP form includes additional information on poster session participation and on submitting proposals to present a session at the symposium.
 

 

2026 Faculty Research Symposium (RSVP)

 

 

Call for Poster Presentations: 2026 N.C. A&T Faculty Research Symposium

 

The poster session will showcase both funded and unfunded research projects that demonstrate innovation, interdisciplinary collaboration, and real-world relevance. Faculty posters will highlight how N.C. A&T research advances knowledge, builds strategic partnerships, and contributes to economic development locally, statewide, and beyond.

Faculty interested in presenting a poster should submit an Intent to Participate through their college’s CompetitionSpace, which opened this week. Each college will coordinate its internal selection process through the associate dean for Research.

Eligibility & Poster Requirements:

  • Eligible Presenters: Faculty and Postdoctoral Scholars may present.
  • Original Work Requirement: Presenters must present their own original research.
  • Poster Size Specifications: Posters must be 36 inches wide × 48 inches high (portrait) or 48 inches wide × 36 inches high (landscape).

Poster Capacity & Timeline

A total of 135 posters will be displayed at the symposium:

  • 16 posters per academic college
  • 5 posters from the Graduate College
  • 2 posters from the Honors College

Final poster selections must be submitted to the Division of Research by March 31, 2026, to support scheduling, poster placement, and logistics.

Additional details regarding poster formatting, judging criteria, and symposium logistics will be shared as the event approaches.

We encourage faculty across all disciplines to participate and help showcase the depth, breadth, and impact of research at N.C. A&T.
