CANVAS FALL 2026

 

North Carolina A&T continues to make significant progress in transitioning to Canvas as the university’s new learning management system, with full implementation scheduled for Fall 2026. 

All courses will be delivered through Canvas beginning Fall 2026, with Summer II 2026 serving as the final term using Blackboard. Blackboard will remain accessible through June 30, 2027, allowing time to retrieve course materials and download important student data such as grades and assignments.

The Instructional Technology Training and Development (ITTD) team is offering live Canvas training sessions available through the monthly training calendar in Cornerstone. In addition, faculty can take advantage of on-demand, self-paced training in Canvas, including recommended courses such as Canvas Basics and Fundamentals of Accessible Digital Content, as well as more in-depth modules on course creation, assignments, grading, and assessments.

Instructional Designers remain available to support faculty with course design and instructional strategies. For additional assistance, faculty may contact tolewis@ncat.edu, bessien@ncat.edu, their assigned Instructional Designer, or ITTD directly at 336-285-4499.

For ongoing updates, resources, and support, visit and bookmark the Canvas Implementation Website. Faculty are encouraged to make full use of these tools and training opportunities to ensure a smooth, successful transition to Canvas.

  • The Canvas LMS Implementation website is located here:  https://hub.ncat.edu/administration/its/ittd/canvas-implementation.php 
  • The Deese College has an assigned instructional designer, David Whitehead (dfwhitehead@ncat.edu or 285-4488), to assist with implementation questions.  


ITTD will hold "Office Hours" in the Deese College on Friday, March 27th, starting at 1:15 PM in the Faculty Lounge, to answer any Canvas or Blackboard accessibility questions. Stop by and engage with our ITTD partners!

Honda Scrum Training
The BISA Department celebrates the successful completion of Part 1: Become a Certified Scrum Master in the Scrum and AI: Agile Training for the Future Workforce program. Since December 2025, more than 100 students and several faculty members have completed the training, gaining foundational knowledge in Agile, Scrum, and the growing role of AI in project environments.  Supported by a Honda CSR grant awarded to Drs. Pankaj Chaudhary, Lavlin Agrawal, and Richelle DaSouza, the program provides hands-on learning that strengthens career-ready skills and aligns with industry needs in data analytics, automation, and innovation.


Dr. Luba Kurkalova
Dr. Kurkalova is co-leading two major research projects totaling over $2 million, providing North Carolina A&T economics students with hands-on, interdisciplinary experience.

She contributes to a $1.17 million NASA-funded project on coastal risk assessment, where she leads the economics component and mentors students in ArcGIS and geospatial analysis to study how flooding impacts economic decisions. She also co-leads a $1 million U.S. Department of Energy project exploring methane-based technologies and their potential for economic development in North Carolina’s coastal regions. In this work, students use Microsoft Power BI to build interactive tools that evaluate potential manufacturing sites.

Through both initiatives, Dr. Kurkalova equips students with advanced technical skills beyond the traditional economics curriculum.


Dr. Meg Weber

Congratulations to Dr. Meg Weber on her outstanding achievements at USASBE 2026! She was honored with the Best Empirical Research Paper award for her work, “It’s Not You, It’s the Room: Understanding Impostor Feelings Through the Lens of Capital Exchange,” co-authored with Dr. Howard Haines. In addition, Dr. Weber received the Minority & Women in Entrepreneurship SIG Emerging Scholar Award.

 

 

 

KPMG Faculty Webinar Series
March 31, 2026
1 PM ET 

Recalibrating the Curriculum for AI-Driven Jobs

We’re excited to invite you to the upcoming session of the KPMG Faculty Webinar Series. Held quarterly, these virtual events offer a unique opportunity for faculty members to connect with KPMG professionals and peers from universities across the country. Together, we will explore emerging topics and foster meaningful dialogue within the academic and professional communities.

 

This one hour virtual session will explore how artificial intelligence is reshaping academic work, student support, and institutional operations across higher education, supporting learners from campus to career.  Drawing on emerging use cases from universities nationwide, the session will provide faculty members with insights into how GenAI tools are being integrated into teaching, advising, and administrative processes. Participants will examine how AI can streamline routine tasks, enhance course design, personalize student learning, and support more data driven decision making—while reinforcing the importance of human oversight.

https://events-meetings.kpmg.com/event/d9d08b8e-4100-4889-aa58-72f7a5c90aef/summary?tm=ThaVIKtfGhrxAVaguHTjiesIX7RYP-j9UN0MA0h7P34

Registration will remain open until March 27.

 Session Speakers

Hear from KPMG Advisory professionals on AI integration in Higher Education, including:

  • Saravanan Subbarayan – Managing Director, Advisory National Technology Leader - Higher Education
  • Ali Stofle – Manager, Advisory – State & Local Operations
  • Rory Smith – Associate, Advisory – State & Local Operations

Learning Objectives

After attending this webinar, participants will be able to:

  • Recognize the role of AI in shaping the future workforce and evolving industry expectations.
  • Examine the impact of AI on knowledge work today, including entry level and managerial roles, through real world experience sharing.
  • Identify key archetypes of AI integration across educational settings.
  • Apply practical strategies for integrating AI into curriculum design and classroom practice.
