Summer Academic Advising Redesign Initiative - Response due by Monday, March 30, 2026
 

The Vice Provost for Undergraduate Programs and Student Success is launching the Summer Academic Advising Redesign Initiative, a strategic effort to establish a comprehensive, standardized advising framework that strengthens the quality, consistency, and effectiveness of advising across all colleges.

The Deese College has the opportunity to nominate up to three (3) individuals who are U.S. Citizens for this initiative.  Selected faculty will contribute to developing advising competencies, standardized practices, training modules, and shared tools that advance proactive, student-centered advising. The Initiative participation details are below:

  • Stipend: $2,500 per faculty participant
  • Timeline: Summer I Session (May 18 – June 23)
  • Time Commitment:
    • Weekly participation in structured sessions (2 hours per session)
    • Meetings will occur 2 to 3 days per week
    • Additional time for collaborative and independent work may be required

Harvard Business Publishing Teaching with Cases Seminar - Response due by Wednesday, April 1, 2026 
 

On behalf of Harvard Business Publishing and Harvard University’s ongoing efforts to build enduring partnerships with HBCUs, we are pleased to invite your institution to participate in the upcoming Teaching with Cases seminar at Harvard Business School.  Now in its third year, this program is designed as a professional development opportunity for business faculty, with a focus on strengthening teaching practices that help students develop critical thinking and decision-making skills that will serve them throughout their academic and professional lives. 

We are seeking to include HBCU business faculty who are interested in using the case method to create engaging, student-centered learning environments and who can share these approaches with colleagues on their campus.


Seminar Details:

  • Dates: July 22 – July 24, 2026
  • Location: Harvard Business School, Boston, MA
  • Facilitators: V.G. Narayanan, Thomas D. Casserly, Jr. Professor of Business Administration and Senior Associate Dean at Harvard Business School; and Alexandra Sedlovskaya, Associate Director of the Christensen Center for Teaching and Learning
  • Costs: Supported by Harvard Business Publishing and Harvard Business School
